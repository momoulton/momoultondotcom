Projects

  • The World of Dorothy Sayers: A Portrait of an Artist.

    Written in HTML5 using D3.js and Google Maps, this site explores the life of novelist, poet, and translator Dorothy L. Sayers (1893-1957). It emphasizes context: social context, geographical context, literary context, historical context. No artist is an island; by providing visual means to represent the contexts in which a given artist lived and work, we can explore the connections that shaped a body of work.

  • Geo Text Analyzer.

    Written in Java, this program allows users to analyze a corpus of texts. It produces comma-separated-variable output files containing frequencies of customized lists of words and place names. The place name files can easily be used to build maps. Download / Source Code

  • Book Club Selector.

    This program uses preferential voting to help groups of friends decide on which book to read for a book club (coming soon...)

  • Literature and Empire.

    This map is built through ArcGIS Online and based on data generated by the GeoText Analyzer. It explores the range of places mentioned in classic 19th century British literature.

  • The Road to Netherne.

    An ArcGIS Online Story Map based on the life of Sarah Hill (1858-1911), as described in Alison Light's Common People: The Story of an English Family (2014)

  • Refugees from the Anglo-Irish War.

    ArcGIS Online web application that shows addresses of a sample of English addresses of individuals making claims for compensation to the UK government for damages incurred for loyalty during the Anglo-Irish War.
